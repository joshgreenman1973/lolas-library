This week's picks

May 29, 2026

A pygmy-shrew detective on the case of some missing pearls, a Louisiana girl tending a crane's egg while her father is trapped in a collapsed mine, a kid finding her footing among bear dogs in the Montana wilderness, a motocross racer told in verse, and a Norse-treasure hunt across small-town Minnesota. Five fresh middle-grade novels from the past two weeks — four of them carrying starred reviews.

1

The Shrew Detective: The Case of the Pilfered Pearls

Margi Preus · illustrated by Junyi Wu
Mystery / Animal fantasy Ages 8–12 Starred · Publishers Weekly

Minerva is a pygmy shrew with a giant vocabulary and a real knack for deduction, so when her cousin calls her in to crack a baffling case, she takes it. A string of pearls has vanished, the homeowner is convinced rodents are to blame, and an exterminator is on the way — which means Minerva has to solve the crime fast to save every small creature in the house. The first book in a new series.

For Lola: If you love a sleuth who wins with brains and a sharp turn of phrase, meet your new favorite tiny detective. It's a cozy, witty whodunit and the start of a series, so there will be more cases to crack.
May 2026 · Amulet Books · Starred reviews · By two-time Newbery Honor author Margi Preus
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

A Wish with Wings

Sarah Guillory
Contemporary Ages 8–12 3 starred reviews

Twelve-year-old Evan loves the wild outdoors of her small Louisiana town — until a sinkhole collapses the local mine and traps her father along with 47 other workers. While the whole town waits and hopes, Evan quietly cares for an abandoned whooping-crane egg as her own act of faith. All the while she carries a heavy secret: she may know what caused the cave-in.

For Lola: This one will wreck you in the best way — a brave, nature-loving heroine and real stakes about courage, guilt and hope. If you tore through Katherine Applegate, this is your next read.
May 19, 2026 · Roaring Brook Press · 3 starred reviews (Kirkus, Booklist, Publishers Weekly)
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

Kestrel Takes Flight

Joy McCullough
Contemporary / Coming of age Ages 9–14 Starred · The Horn Book

Eleven-year-old Kestrel is uprooted from San Diego to Bozeman, Montana, where her mom takes a job at a bear institute caring for Karelian bear dogs. Afraid of the dogs, of the wide-open wilderness, and of her gruff grandfather, Kestrel has to find her footing far from everything she knows. Told in spare, fast-moving verse, it's a story about fear, freedom and figuring out where home really is.

For Lola: A big-hearted story you can read in one sitting, about a girl learning to trust herself in a place that scares her. If you love animals and a heroine who grows brave on her own terms, this one's for you.
May 26, 2026 · Atheneum · Starred review (The Horn Book) · By acclaimed author Joy McCullough
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

Eva to the Max

Rebecca Caprara
Sports / Novel in verse Ages 10–14 Starred · Kirkus

Eva is a 12-year-old motocross racer who keeps her dirt-bike passion half-hidden as she juggles competition with school and family. Told in fast, punchy verse, the story follows her facing down doubters and new rivals while figuring out who she wants to be — with real history at the back about how girls fought their way into the sport.

For Lola: A gutsy girl tearing up a track in a sport that keeps underestimating her, told in verse that flies by. Perfect if you like a determined underdog and a story you can finish in an afternoon.
May 19, 2026 · Dial Books · Starred review (Kirkus)
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

Berserkers

M. A. Larson
Fantasy / Adventure Ages 8–12

Danny Hall, a kid from tiny Norse Ridge, Minnesota, gets swept into a quest for lost Viking treasure and a showdown with a band of villainous conquistadors. With a crew of fantastical companions, he tunnels through underground caves and clears one obstacle after another in a fast, funny, Norse-flavored romp.

For Lola: If you've burned through every Rick Riordan book and want more big-myth, big-laughs treasure hunting, this is your next quest. Pure propulsive adventure with monsters, jokes and a world built for getting lost in.
May 26, 2026 · Farrar, Straus and Giroux (BYR) · By the author of Pennyroyal Academy
Buy at Bookshop.org

May 15, 2026

1

Lily Tripp: Diary of an Accidental Time Traveler

Amelia Tait
Time travel / Humor Ages 10–14 Series debut

Every New Year's Eve when the clock strikes midnight, 13-year-old Lily Tripp jumps to a different century — ancient Rome one year, the 1920s the next. Her cat, her best friend Joey, and her terrible crush Ollie keep landing in each era with her, in new bodies and new lives. The book is Lily's diary as she tries to figure out the rules of her own time-traveling life. The start of a trilogy.

For Lola: Back to the Future meets Judy Blume. Lily is funny and self-aware and a little embarrassing in the exact right way, and you'll learn more weird history than you expect.
May 12, 2026 · Farrar, Straus and Giroux (BYR) · First in a trilogy
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

The Edge of Forever

Meghan P. Browne
Contemporary / Mystery Ages 9–12

Still reeling from her dad's death, Maisie gets dropped off with Aunt Gertie for the summer in the tiny town of Heaven, Texas. She and Walt, the boy next door, take on odd jobs around town — and stumble onto a quiet plan to develop one of Heaven's most beloved natural spots. The deeper they dig, the closer they get to a long-buried secret about Maisie's own family.

For Lola: A summer-in-a-small-town story with real teeth. Grief, friendship, a county fair, and a mystery that turns out to be way more personal than Maisie wants it to be.
May 12, 2026 · Feiwel & Friends · 288 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

The Strange Disappearance of Imogen Good

Kirsty Applebaum
Mystery / Fantasy Ages 9–13

Fran gets shipped off to her aunt and uncle's cottage on the grounds of Stillness Hall while her parents travel for work. She's braced for her snooty cousin Imogen — only Imogen isn't there. Her room is exactly as she left it, photos are up on every wall, but nobody will admit she ever existed. Woven through Fran's investigation is an old tale called "The Twelve Statues of Stillness Hall," and a forbidden, overgrown garden no one is supposed to enter.

For Lola: Gothic and quietly spooky in the best way. A creepy old estate, a missing girl no one will name, and a story-within-a-story that slowly clicks into place.
May 5, 2026 · Nosy Crow
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

The Mystery of the Stolen World Cup Trophy

Angela Cervantes
Mystery / Sports Ages 8–12 Starred · Kirkus

Twelve-year-old Diez Espada is a kid detective working a real case: the World Cup trophy has been stolen, and the championship match is days away. The investigation takes him through stadiums, hotels, and locker rooms filled with cameos from real soccer legends, and forces him to figure out who, exactly, he can trust on a global stage.

For Lola: You do not need to know a thing about soccer. Diez is a wry, watchful narrator, and the mystery actually plays fair — you can solve it along with him if you pay attention.
May 5, 2026 · Roaring Brook · Starred review (Kirkus)
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

Our Fair Share

Sarah Marie Jette
Contemporary / Friendship Ages 9–12

James, Avory, Cassi, and Seraphina have spent every summer working their small Maine fair. This year, each of them is hiding something heavy — a new school that isn't going well, a family scandal, a quiet accident, a feeling of being invisible. Then a new fair manager arrives with plans that could gut everything they love about the place, and the four of them have to decide whether to fight for it together.

For Lola: Four narrators, four very different inner lives, one summer. The fair itself becomes a character — cotton candy, livestock barns, demolition derby and all — and the friendships feel earned, not easy.
May 12, 2026 · Henry Holt (BYR) · 320 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org

April 17, 2026

1

The Genie Game

Jordan Ifueko
Fantasy / Dystopian Ages 10–14 Starred · Publishers Weekly

Valentine Adesanya wants two things: to become a Feared and Fabulous Film Director, and to find her missing big sister Mango. She suspects the Trio Trust — the creepy mega-companies that now run the United States — made Mango disappear. Then a text lures Valentine into a magical boba shop, where she learns she has just been drafted as a Genie: part of an underground workforce of immortal engineers who grant mortals' wishes while the Trio collects the magic.

For Lola: From the author of Raybearer. Equal parts Black Mirror, fantasy epic, and wish-granting game show. The start of a series — get in on the ground floor.
April 21, 2026 · Amulet · Starred review (Publishers Weekly)
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

Where Ella Went

Laurie Morrison
Mystery / Contemporary Ages 10–14

Eighth-graders Sadie and Pug have nothing in common except soccer and their loyalty to Ella, their overachieving team captain. When Ella doesn't show up for the biggest game of the season and her parents pull her out of school, Sadie and Pug team up to figure out what happened — even though they can barely stand each other — piecing the story together from recordings, texts, journal entries, and emails.

For Lola: Told entirely in documents, so the format alone is a puzzle. A sharp, funny mystery about friendship and the weird pressure cooker of eighth-grade sports.
April 14, 2026 · Amulet · 304 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

Confessions of a Mango

Kate Lumsden & Nate Pieplow
Contemporary Ages 8–12

Ruby Emmerson has always felt like the "dumb twin." She has dyslexia, dysgraphia, and dyscalculia, and she's drowning at her competitive charter school while her brother Bryce aces everything. So Ruby starts an anonymous online account as "Mango" — an outsider looking in — and discovers she isn't nearly as alone as she thought.

For Lola: For anyone who's ever felt like the one person in the room who doesn't get it. In the spirit of Fish in a Tree and Frindle, but with its own funny, anxious voice.
April 14, 2026 · Little, Brown · 224 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

Clock Hands

Marieke Nijkamp · illustrated by Sylvia Bi
Graphic novel / Historical fantasy Ages 10–14

In the Renaissance-inspired city of Siannerra, the trade guilds rule and the apprenticeship fees are impossible. Vale, a nonbinary kid from a guildless family, scrapes together work on the docks dreaming of metalwork. Then Maestro Giuseppe arrives to build the city's first astronomical clock — and he doesn't care about fees. A companion to the acclaimed Ink Girls.

For Lola: Gorgeous art, real tension about who gets to make things, and a big reveal about the guilds. If you liked Ink Girls, this one's even better.
April 21, 2026 · Greenwillow · 288 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

The Lions' Run

Sara Pennypacker · illustrated by Jon Klassen
Historical fiction Ages 10–14 NYT bestseller · 3 starred reviews

Lucas DuBois is tired of his cowardly reputation and tired of the Nazis occupying his French village. When he rescues a litter of kittens from some bullies and hides them in an abandoned stable, it pulls him into the work of the Resistance — and forces him to figure out what kind of person he really wants to be. From the author of Pax, with art by Caldecott winner Jon Klassen.

For Lola: A few months old but too good to skip. Starred by Horn Book, Publishers Weekly, and Booklist. If you loved Pax, this one will wreck you in the best way.
Feb. 3, 2026 · Balzer + Bray · 288 pp. · 3 starred reviews
Buy at Bookshop.org

1

Found Sound

Meg Wolitzer & Charlie Panek
Mystery / STEM Ages 8–12 Starred · Kirkus

Felix's summers in Blissfield haven't been the same since his big brother left home. Then he and his neighbor Marigold find a mysterious box labeled OPEN ME, stuffed with an audio recorder and a cryptic message that sends them on a sound-themed treasure hunt across town.

For Lola: Part puzzle mystery, part crash course in how sound actually works — reverb, anechoic chambers, the whole deal. You'll want to listen to everything differently after this one.
April 21, 2026 · Dutton · Starred review (Kirkus)
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

When You're Brave Enough

Rebecca Bendheim
Contemporary Ages 10–14 Starred · Publishers Weekly

Eighth-grader Lacey moves across the country, away from her lifelong best friend Grace. At her new theater-arts magnet school in Rhode Island, she lands the lead in the musical, preps for her bat mitzvah, and starts figuring out who she is when she's not standing in someone else's shadow.

For Lola: Honest and funny about all the stuff that's actually hard in eighth grade — new friendships, first crushes, and the terrifying freedom of getting to be yourself.
April 7, 2026 · Viking · Starred review (Publishers Weekly)
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

Olive Oakes and the Haunted Carousel

Kalynn Bayron
Mystery / Horror Ages 8–12 Bestselling author

Olive Oakes carries a notebook everywhere, ready to record anything suspicious. When her family visits Whispering Woods, she hears about a traveling carnival with a haunted carousel, missing kids, and ghost sightings — and decides to investigate.

For Lola: Think Nancy Drew meets a ghost story. Olive is the kind of detective who actually writes everything down, and the carousel scenes are genuinely creepy.
April 7, 2026 · Bloomsbury
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

Nell O'Dell Hates Quests

Emily B. Martin
Fantasy / Humor Ages 8–12

Nell is the daughter of legendary adventurers, but she'd rather tend her garden at the family inn than go on any quest, thank you very much. When the inn's deed is stolen, she reluctantly sets out with Midge, a dramatic goblin, and Bevin, a soft-spoken troll with a talent for herbcraft.

For Lola: A fantasy that makes fun of fantasy — but also totally works as one. Nell's companions are unforgettable, and the whole thing has huge cozy-adventure energy.
April 7, 2026 · Candlewick
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

The Traitor Moth

Katharine Orton
Epic fantasy Ages 8–12

Catclaw is a young moth and the last of the Dreamkins, a noble clan in exile. When he receives a prophetic vision warning that all five moth clans are in danger, nobody believes him. Branded a traitor and cast out, he sets off alone to save them anyway.

For Lola: Watership Down but with moths. Massive world, high stakes, and a hero who has to earn every ounce of trust. The kind of fantasy you disappear into.
April 7, 2026 · Simon & Schuster

When Tomorrow Burns

Fantasy Ages 10–14 Newbery winner

Three friends discover an ancient prophecy coming true as wildfires sweep through Seattle. From the author of When You Trap a Tiger, a story about what you'd sacrifice for the people you love.

For Lola: Myth meets real-world stakes. If you couldn't put down When You Trap a Tiger, this one goes even bigger.

Mystery on Macaw Mountain

María José Fitzgerald
Mystery Ages 8–12

Four cousins at a bird sanctuary in Honduras have to solve the theft of eight nearly extinct scarlet macaws. The trail leads through downtown Copán and into mist-shrouded Mayan ruins.

For Lola: A real page-turner with a tropical setting, cool animal facts, and cousins who tease each other one minute and save the day the next.

No Way, Never Sisters

Humor Ages 8–12

Melisa and Roxy can't stand each other. So when their parents get engaged, they launch an all-out sabotage campaign. Somewhere between the pranks and the chaos, they realize becoming sisters might not be the worst thing ever.

For Lola: The Parent Trap in reverse. Laugh-out-loud funny, genuinely creative pranks, and two voices that sound nothing alike.

Seventh-grader Luna makes zines with her best friend Scott. When classmates start paying attention, Luna gets swept into the popular crowd — and has to figure out how much of herself she's willing to give up to stay there.

For Lola: Funny, anxious, and so real. Called "today's Are You There God? It's Me, Margaret." The zine-making thread is especially cool.

Contractors find a 1944 painting and a mysterious letter hidden inside a Paris apartment wall. In the present, Penny wants to know who put it there. In 1943, a girl named Marie is trying to survive Nazi-occupied France. Two timelines, one secret.

For Lola: A puzzle-box of a book. If you love art, history, and secrets, this one doesn't let go.
