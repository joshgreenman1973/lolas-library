This week's picks

April 17, 2026

Genies, soccer mysteries, Renaissance clockmakers, and a kid who is tired of being called the "dumb twin" — five new middle-grade novels released this past week and a half, including a couple with starred reviews. Something here for everyone.

1

The Genie Game

Jordan Ifueko
Fantasy / Dystopian Ages 10–14 Starred · Publishers Weekly

Valentine Adesanya wants two things: to become a Feared and Fabulous Film Director, and to find her missing big sister Mango. She suspects the Trio Trust — the creepy mega-companies that now run the United States — made Mango disappear. Then a text lures Valentine into a magical boba shop, where she learns she has just been drafted as a Genie: part of an underground workforce of immortal engineers who grant mortals' wishes while the Trio collects the magic.

For Lola: From the author of Raybearer. Equal parts Black Mirror, fantasy epic, and wish-granting game show. The start of a series — get in on the ground floor.
April 21, 2026 · Amulet · Starred review (Publishers Weekly)
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

Where Ella Went

Laurie Morrison
Mystery / Contemporary Ages 10–14

Eighth-graders Sadie and Pug have nothing in common except soccer and their loyalty to Ella, their overachieving team captain. When Ella doesn't show up for the biggest game of the season and her parents pull her out of school, Sadie and Pug team up to figure out what happened — even though they can barely stand each other — piecing the story together from recordings, texts, journal entries, and emails.

For Lola: Told entirely in documents, so the format alone is a puzzle. A sharp, funny mystery about friendship and the weird pressure cooker of eighth-grade sports.
April 14, 2026 · Amulet · 304 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

Confessions of a Mango

Kate Lumsden & Nate Pieplow
Contemporary Ages 8–12

Ruby Emmerson has always felt like the "dumb twin." She has dyslexia, dysgraphia, and dyscalculia, and she's drowning at her competitive charter school while her brother Bryce aces everything. So Ruby starts an anonymous online account as "Mango" — an outsider looking in — and discovers she isn't nearly as alone as she thought.

For Lola: For anyone who's ever felt like the one person in the room who doesn't get it. In the spirit of Fish in a Tree and Frindle, but with its own funny, anxious voice.
April 14, 2026 · Little, Brown · 224 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

Clock Hands

Marieke Nijkamp · illustrated by Sylvia Bi
Graphic novel / Historical fantasy Ages 10–14

In the Renaissance-inspired city of Siannerra, the trade guilds rule and the apprenticeship fees are impossible. Vale, a nonbinary kid from a guildless family, scrapes together work on the docks dreaming of metalwork. Then Maestro Giuseppe arrives to build the city's first astronomical clock — and he doesn't care about fees. A companion to the acclaimed Ink Girls.

For Lola: Gorgeous art, real tension about who gets to make things, and a big reveal about the guilds. If you liked Ink Girls, this one's even better.
April 21, 2026 · Greenwillow · 288 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

The Lions' Run

Sara Pennypacker · illustrated by Jon Klassen
Historical fiction Ages 10–14 NYT bestseller · 3 starred reviews

Lucas DuBois is tired of his cowardly reputation and tired of the Nazis occupying his French village. When he rescues a litter of kittens from some bullies and hides them in an abandoned stable, it pulls him into the work of the Resistance — and forces him to figure out what kind of person he really wants to be. From the author of Pax, with art by Caldecott winner Jon Klassen.

For Lola: A few months old but too good to skip. Starred by Horn Book, Publishers Weekly, and Booklist. If you loved Pax, this one will wreck you in the best way.
Feb. 3, 2026 · Balzer + Bray · 288 pp. · 3 starred reviews
Buy at Bookshop.org

April 11, 2026

1

Found Sound

Meg Wolitzer & Charlie Panek
Mystery / STEM Ages 8–12 Starred · Kirkus

Felix's summers in Blissfield haven't been the same since his big brother left home. Then he and his neighbor Marigold find a mysterious box labeled OPEN ME, stuffed with an audio recorder and a cryptic message that sends them on a sound-themed treasure hunt across town.

For Lola: Part puzzle mystery, part crash course in how sound actually works — reverb, anechoic chambers, the whole deal. You'll want to listen to everything differently after this one.
April 21, 2026 · Dutton · Starred review (Kirkus)
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

When You're Brave Enough

Rebecca Bendheim
Contemporary Ages 10–14 Starred · Publishers Weekly

Eighth-grader Lacey moves across the country, away from her lifelong best friend Grace. At her new theater-arts magnet school in Rhode Island, she lands the lead in the musical, preps for her bat mitzvah, and starts figuring out who she is when she's not standing in someone else's shadow.

For Lola: Honest and funny about all the stuff that's actually hard in eighth grade — new friendships, first crushes, and the terrifying freedom of getting to be yourself.
April 7, 2026 · Viking · Starred review (Publishers Weekly)
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

Olive Oakes and the Haunted Carousel

Kalynn Bayron
Mystery / Horror Ages 8–12 Bestselling author

Olive Oakes carries a notebook everywhere, ready to record anything suspicious. When her family visits Whispering Woods, she hears about a traveling carnival with a haunted carousel, missing kids, and ghost sightings — and decides to investigate.

For Lola: Think Nancy Drew meets a ghost story. Olive is the kind of detective who actually writes everything down, and the carousel scenes are genuinely creepy.
April 7, 2026 · Bloomsbury
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

Nell O'Dell Hates Quests

Emily B. Martin
Fantasy / Humor Ages 8–12

Nell is the daughter of legendary adventurers, but she'd rather tend her garden at the family inn than go on any quest, thank you very much. When the inn's deed is stolen, she reluctantly sets out with Midge, a dramatic goblin, and Bevin, a soft-spoken troll with a talent for herbcraft.

For Lola: A fantasy that makes fun of fantasy — but also totally works as one. Nell's companions are unforgettable, and the whole thing has huge cozy-adventure energy.
April 7, 2026 · Candlewick
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

The Traitor Moth

Katharine Orton
Epic fantasy Ages 8–12

Catclaw is a young moth and the last of the Dreamkins, a noble clan in exile. When he receives a prophetic vision warning that all five moth clans are in danger, nobody believes him. Branded a traitor and cast out, he sets off alone to save them anyway.

For Lola: Watership Down but with moths. Massive world, high stakes, and a hero who has to earn every ounce of trust. The kind of fantasy you disappear into.
April 7, 2026 · Simon & Schuster
Buy at Bookshop.org

March 13, 2026

1

When Tomorrow Burns

Tae Keller
Fantasy Ages 10–14 Newbery winner

Three friends discover an ancient prophecy coming true as wildfires sweep through Seattle. From the author of When You Trap a Tiger, a story about what you'd sacrifice for the people you love.

For Lola: Myth meets real-world stakes. If you couldn't put down When You Trap a Tiger, this one goes even bigger.
March 3, 2026 · Random House · 272 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
2

Mystery on Macaw Mountain

María José Fitzgerald
Mystery Ages 8–12

Four cousins at a bird sanctuary in Honduras have to solve the theft of eight nearly extinct scarlet macaws. The trail leads through downtown Copán and into mist-shrouded Mayan ruins.

For Lola: A real page-turner with a tropical setting, cool animal facts, and cousins who tease each other one minute and save the day the next.
March 10, 2026 · Knopf · 272 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
3

No Way, Never Sisters

Chantel Acevedo & Natalia Sylvester
Humor Ages 8–12

Melisa and Roxy can't stand each other. So when their parents get engaged, they launch an all-out sabotage campaign. Somewhere between the pranks and the chaos, they realize becoming sisters might not be the worst thing ever.

For Lola: The Parent Trap in reverse. Laugh-out-loud funny, genuinely creative pranks, and two voices that sound nothing alike.
March 10, 2026 · Aladdin · 288 pp.
Buy at Bookshop.org
4

The Moon Without Stars

Chanel Miller
Realistic fiction Ages 10–14 Starred · NYT bestseller

Seventh-grader Luna makes zines with her best friend Scott. When classmates start paying attention, Luna gets swept into the popular crowd — and has to figure out how much of herself she's willing to give up to stay there.

For Lola: Funny, anxious, and so real. Called "today's Are You There God? It's Me, Margaret." The zine-making thread is especially cool.
Jan. 13, 2026 · Philomel · Starred review (The Horn Book)
Buy at Bookshop.org
5

Each and Every Spark

Claire Swinarski
Historical / Mystery Ages 10–13 Edgar-nominated author

Contractors find a 1944 painting and a mysterious letter hidden inside a Paris apartment wall. In the present, Penny wants to know who put it there. In 1943, a girl named Marie is trying to survive Nazi-occupied France. Two timelines, one secret.

For Lola: A puzzle-box of a book. If you love art, history, and secrets, this one doesn't let go.
Feb. 17, 2026 · Quill Tree Books
Buy at Bookshop.org
